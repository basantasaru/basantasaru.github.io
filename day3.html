📝 Basanta’s Daily Journal – November 3, 2025

🌞 Journal Entry

Today, I woke up around 7:30 AM with body pain from yesterday’s work. After waking up, I went outside, started working, and drank some water. The weather looked clear—it didn’t seem like it would rain today. So, I had to put the rice seeds out in the sun to dry, because if they start growing, we can’t eat them.

My dad went somewhere, so I had to do most of the hard work. My mom was busy cooking in the kitchen, and later, when she got some time, she helped me with the work. I was carrying wet grains, which was quite heavy. I got hungry and ate my morning meal, then continued working without any rest.

At one point, my mom wasn’t listening to me, and I got angry. I even had a thought of damaging the plants, but I controlled my anger—something I’ve learned from being a devotee of Lord Shiva and following the example of Lord Shree Ram. I reminded myself that it was my duty to finish the work, so I kept going despite being angry.

By 11 AM, I finished the main work and took a 30-minute rest. Then I continued working again. Later, I came home, rested a bit, and went to wash clothes. I started feeling dizzy and weak, so I returned home, ate, and rested.

My mom went to cut grass, and my dad came home around 3 PM while I was sleeping. I told him to wash my clothes. After an hour, he woke me up and told me to get them, but when I went there, I saw nothing and thought he had already brought them back since I couldn’t see them. When he saw me still lying in bed, he got angry and started shouting at me.

I felt very angry, shocked, and confused. I had worked hard both yesterday and today, but my dad didn’t even think about that. I didn’t know how to react—whether to explain, laugh, or fight back. Still, I got up, went to the river, and found the clothes folded and not easily visible. I brought them home, then covered all the grains with a plastic tent to protect them.

After finishing all that, I came home and worked on improving my webpage. I added many new things with the help of AI. Today, I felt weak and powerless. Since November 1, I’ve stopped eating meat, and lately, I’ve been feeling a bit dizzy and lightheaded.

So, that’s how my day went.

🌿 What I Learned Today

  • Patience controls power – Getting angry doesn’t solve anything; staying calm helps finish the work faster and keeps peace in the family.
  • Duty before emotion – Even when I was tired or upset, I kept working because responsibility comes first.
  • Respect energy limits – My body felt weak today; it reminded me that rest and good food are also part of discipline.
  • Misunderstandings happen – My dad’s anger wasn’t about hate, but about misunderstanding. Next time, I’ll respond with calm explanation, not reaction.
  • Devotion brings control – Remembering Lord Shiva and Lord Ram helped me master my anger. Spiritual strength is real power.
  • Progress comes in small steps – Even after a long, hard day, I still improved my website. That’s how consistency builds growth.
