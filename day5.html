Daily Diary – Basanta

Date: November 5

Today I woke up early and went to my “kaile xamai’s” house to help carry water from the padhera. When I reached at 5:30 AM, they were still sleeping, so I returned to “maile xamai’s” house and went again after 6 AM.

But they didn’t let me work. I stayed there, talked for a while, and then went to kanchi xamai’s house. Today is my big brother’s (xamai’s son) Paachawali, so I stayed there since yesterday.

Since I couldn’t do heavy work today, I helped with small tasks. At 11 AM, we went to the Kaal Bhairav temple for puja by jeep. We reached in one hour and did the rituals, but inside I felt very sad seeing the sacrifice (bali) of innocent animals.

I cried inside and had many questions for God. There was a goat from our home and a buffalo. There were also two pigeons and one rooster.

In the middle of all this, I got a message from a girl. I don’t know if it was blessing, teaching, or testing — LOL. After rituals, we returned in half an hour.

She messaged again, but I was helping in small work. Sometimes I was busy, sometimes free. She said, “You say you’re busy, but you’re still using your phone.” I didn’t want to ignore her, so I replied. Later I forgot to reply while talking with xamai.

In the evening, when I went to see the animals, the buffalo was gone. I realized it was the same one I had been petting in the morning. It made painful sounds… I cried inside, but couldn’t cry outside.

Later, I watched Krishna Aur Kans movie on YouTube with my little sister Deepika and her friend. I watched it when I was young, but now it felt wonderful because I know more about Hinduism.

We ate and then went to Ramilo, where people were dancing. One person danced so funny that it became a memorable moment.

At 9 PM, I was heading to bed. She messaged again. During the conversation, she told me to make her my girlfriend, so I said yes. I finished talking and slept before 10 PM.

What I Learned Today

  • Compassion matters: Seeing animal sacrifice showed me how strongly I care about kindness to all living beings.
  • Emotional strength: Sometimes we stay strong outside even when we are crying inside.
  • Spiritual knowledge makes life meaningful: When we learn, small things start having big meaning.
  • Life gives surprises: New people, unexpected moments — anything can happen anytime.
  • Discipline is power: Even with emotions and distractions, I slept before 10 PM.

So this much for today.
